💧 Water Quality & Parameters

  • Test water weekly — Ammonia 0 ppm, Nitrite 0 ppm, Nitrate below 20 ppm, pH 6.5–7.5 for most tropical fish
  • Perform 20–30% water changes every week to maintain stable parameters
  • Always use a dechlorinator before adding tap water to the tank
  • Cycle your tank for 4–6 weeks before adding fish — this establishes beneficial bacteria
  • Avoid sudden parameter swings; change water gradually and match temperature
  • Use a strong biological filter rated for at least 2× your tank volume
  • Never use soap or detergent to clean tank equipment

🌡 Temperature & Environment

  • Tropical fish: 24–28°C. Goldfish: 18–22°C. Discus: 28–30°C
  • Use an aquarium heater and a separate thermometer to verify temperature accuracy
  • Keep the tank away from direct sunlight, air-conditioning vents, and windows
  • Maintain good surface agitation for oxygen exchange — filter return or air stone
  • Add caves, plants, and driftwood to provide hiding spots and reduce stress
  • Avoid temperature fluctuations of more than 1–2°C in a single day

🍽 Feeding & Nutrition

  • Feed small amounts 1–2 times daily — only what fish can consume in 2 minutes
  • Uneaten food causes ammonia spikes; remove any leftover food promptly
  • Vary the diet: alternate between flakes, pellets, and frozen/live foods like bloodworms
  • Fast your fish one day per week to prevent constipation and overfeeding-related issues
  • Herbivorous fish (plecos, some cichlids) also need blanched vegetables like zucchini
  • Bettas are carnivores — feed betta-specific pellets, not generic flakes

🆕 Introducing New Fish

  • Float the unopened transport bag in your tank for 15–20 minutes to equalise temperature
  • Drip-acclimate slowly by adding tank water to the bag every 5 minutes over 30 minutes
  • Do NOT pour bag water into your tank — it may contain pathogens or poor-quality water
  • Quarantine new fish in a separate tank for 2–4 weeks before introducing to your main tank
  • Dim the lights for the first few hours after introduction to reduce stress
  • Feed lightly for the first 48 hours — new fish are often stressed and will not eat immediately

🌿 Live Plants

  • Live plants improve oxygen levels, absorb nitrates, and reduce algae by competing for nutrients
  • Beginner-friendly plants: Java Fern, Anubias, Java Moss, Hornwort (no specialist lighting needed)
  • Plants need light 8–10 hours/day — use a timer for consistency
  • Use liquid fertiliser or root tabs if plants show yellowing leaves
  • Trim regularly to prevent overgrowth and maintain good water circulation
  • Rinse plants thoroughly before adding to tank to remove snails or pests

🏥 Disease Prevention & Treatment

  • Ich (white spots): Raise temperature to 28°C and treat with aquarium salt or ich medication
  • Fin Rot: Usually caused by poor water quality. Improve water conditions, use antibacterial treatment
  • Velvet (gold dust appearance): Highly contagious. Dim lights, treat with copper-based medication
  • Prevention: maintain good water quality, quarantine new arrivals, avoid overcrowding
  • Keep a hospital tank ready for sick fish — treating in the main tank affects beneficial bacteria
  • Never use medications beyond the recommended dose — overdosing can kill fish
  • Do not mix medications without professional advice

🤝 Fish Compatibility

  • Never keep two male Betta fish together — they will fight to the death
  • Avoid mixing fin-nippers (tiger barbs, serpae tetras) with slow or long-finned fish
  • Research adult size before buying — a small "community" fish may grow large enough to eat others
  • Match temperature requirements — do not mix goldfish (cool water) with tropical fish
  • Add new fish gradually to avoid overwhelming the tank's biological filtration
  • Provide territory and hiding places to reduce aggression among cichlids and other territorial species

🚨 Emergency Situations

  • Fish gasping at surface: Low dissolved oxygen. Add air stone, do partial water change immediately
  • Cloudy water: Bacterial bloom — usually harmless but do a partial water change and check filter
  • Green water: Algae bloom from too much light or nutrients. Reduce light hours and do a water change
  • Fish hiding constantly: Sign of stress or illness. Check water parameters and look for bullying
  • Power outage: Use a battery-powered air pump to maintain oxygen. Act fast in hot weather
  • Fish floating sideways: Swim bladder issue. Fast the fish for 48 hours, then feed a blanched pea
